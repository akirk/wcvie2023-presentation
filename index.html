What is the Fediverse?

  • Webservices that are based on Federation
  • Federation: someone else hosts your data but it's our choice who that someone is
  • Instead of a single well known service name, you need to know your server
  • Servers communicate among each other to enable the same experience as centralized services

Mastering the Fediverse

  1. Share to the Fediverse

1. Share to the Fediverse

  • Use the Fediverse as a multiplicator
  • Write a post on WordPress, share to the Fediverse
  • Many plugins: Mastodon Auto Share, Mastodon Autopost, Share on Mastodon
  • Experience like Twitter: You connect your WordPress with your Mastodon Account

1. Share to the Fediverse

  • When you post something, a post ("note") on your Mastodon Account is created
  • Typically: Title + URL
  • → that note will be shared with all your followers on Mastodon
  • The sender is your Mastodon account
  • Your account is on a hosted Mastodon server

2. Be part of the Fediverse

  • Make your WordPress speak the same protocol as Mastodon: ActivityPub
  • ActivityPub plugin
  • Your WordPress post is your post on Mastodon
  • People don't follow your Mastodon account but they follow your blog
  • Your identity = username@yourblog

2. Be part of the Fediverse

  • People directly follow your WordPress from Mastodon
  • When you post something, it directly sends your post to all your followers
  • Thus, WordPress keeps track of your followers
  • The posts come from username@yourblog
  • You use Mastodon to follow others and write status posts

Mastodon Experience

  • Many Mastodon clients exist on many platforms
  • You can use them with your own WordPress
  • Enable Mastodon Apps plugin
  • Makes your blog talk the Mastodon API
  • Tusky, Ivory, Pinacore, Elk, Mona, ...

Mastodon Experience

ActivityPub plugin

  • People from Mastodon directly follow your blog
  • Your blog can be followed like a Mastodon account
  • When someone replies, it arrives at your blog as comment to the post
  • Your blog maintains your list of followers
  • https://wordpress.org/plugins/activitypub

Friends plugin

  • Mission: Your WordPress is your online identity
  • People focussed: multiple RSS/ActivityPub feeds per person
  • Private post sharing just with connected blogs
  • Save full articles → send them to your E-Reader
  • https://wordpress.org/plugins/friends

Enable Mastodon Apps plugin

  • Implements the same API as the real Mastodon
  • In Mastodon clients you enter your blog URL
  • Not the whole API implemented yet, but usable
  • https://wordpress.org/plugins/enable-mastodon-apps

Mastodon tidbits

  • Mastodon doesn't lock you in: you can move to another server and take your followers along
  • Mastodon cannot do virtual hosts, see #2668
  • you need to host it yourself for a nice address (e.g. with masto.host)
  • A huge software stack → use your own WordPress?

Matrix

  • Real-time chat, end to end encrypted
  • Federated, using homeservers
  • Aims to replace Slack; also WhatsApp, Signal, etc.
  • Multi-messenger with Matrix possible today with Beeper (waitlist)

Matrix and WordPress

  • WordPress.org will switch from Slack to Matrix
  • Embed a Matrix chat directly on your own WordPress with Chatrix
  • Gutenberg block around the Hydrogen client
  • Can be restricted to a single room
  • WordPress.org: SSO to use your own WordPress account as an account on Matrix
